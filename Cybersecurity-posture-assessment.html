Detection of exploitable vulnerabilities that can compromise your business and suggest/implement adequate mitigation controls to significantly lower the business impact possibilities of any cybersecurity incident

Vulnerability and penetration testing (VAPT):

An assessment of client’s current cybersecurity posture will be executed to detect vulnerabilities. It will include one or both of the following

> Technical: analyzing the client IT Infrastructure and users for technical vulnerabilities

> Functional: business process workflow review (gap analysis) to detect. This service is important for client to have enough inputs to build an ISMS (information security management system) to comply with the intranational standards and business requirements.

A report of the findings and suggested mitigation controls will be generated and submitted to client Usually, majority of clients take this only to fulfill compliance needs. We need to promote the other 2 stages so they can have a much more rigid cyber defenses and resiliency.

Cybersecurity Posture Assessment

Cybersecurity posture assessment

The best approach to ensure a cyber resilient work environment is to systematically identify the business requirements and the current gaps to provide enough accurate inputs to make the appropriate decision of establishing a strategic plan for addressing the risks introduced by the founded vulnerabilities in order of effective enhancement of the organization’s cyber resiliency level. Cybersecurity posture assessment services will help organizations strengthen their cybersecurity defences by providing a complete cybersecurity roadmap that complies with the risk assessment, risk treatment and performance evaluation requirement of information security standards.


Why do you need Cyber-Security Posture Assessment?

A majority of organizations are highly dependent on the Internet and networks to run their daily business.

However, an organization is unaware of the security issues that might result in an attack (from outside or from within). Customer information, the organization’s private and confidential data, intellectual property, and information assets might leak out to the public––thereby resulting in huge financial losses and damage to the organization’s reputation.

In order to measure the overall cyber-security maturity of the organization, an independent expert assessment of the current state of its information security environment is conducted against global standards and leading industry practices. It is followed by a remediation of the identified gaps and the development of a roadmap for transformation. After implementation of required mitigation controls, a final vulnerability and penetration test will be executed to assure the effectiveness of implemented measures.


Strategy for improving posture assessment

Cybersecurity posture assessment is one of the most critical aspect for an organization. For improving the posture for our clients, our subject matter experts will be doing the following:

> Identify your inventory in the organization.

>Check the IT assets against all the major threats like phishing, unpatched software, outdated software, viruses, SQL injection, and others.

>Create analytics to draw reference from. You should have a strategy in place where you need to improvise.

>Discovered vulnerabilities will be assigned with a criticality level which is decided on the level of impact it does on the system. Action plan to mitigate those vulnerabilities and best practices suggestions will be reported.

>Assess/implement the required suggested mitigation controls.


Phases involved in Security posture assessment
Planning Phase:

Validating the scope of the assessment, resource identification, stakeholder’s identification, developing a work-plan, etc. happens in this phase.


Documentation review:

All the documents that are required to commence testing will be reviewed in this phase.


Assessment:

Internet exposure, on-site/ off-site audit, findings, analysis and -defining cyber-security posture will be carried over in this phase. Reporting: All the deliverable will be listed in the report.


Tips to improve your cyber-security posture

>Have a real-time updatable inventory of the IT assets of your company

>Continuous monitoring of IT assets and expose the system to planned audits and assess the efficiency of the current controls

>Analyse the result and do a proper risk assessment , mark the discovered vulnerabilities and implement the right control to mitigate them

>Cycle the previous steps periodically for continuous improvements and quality assurance
